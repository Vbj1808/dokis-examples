Aspirin reduces fever by inhibiting COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, which
decreases prostaglandin synthesis in the body. It is widely used as an
analgesic, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory medication. Aspirin also
has antiplatelet properties and is used in low doses to prevent heart
attacks and strokes. Common side effects include gastrointestinal
irritation and increased bleeding risk.
